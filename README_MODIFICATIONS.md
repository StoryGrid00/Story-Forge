# StoryForge - Modified Version

## What's New

This modified version of StoryForge includes significant improvements to the **World Building** section, making it more intuitive and powerful for writers to develop their story worlds.

## Key Improvements

### 1. Cleaner Interface
The duplicate "World Building" header has been removed from the top of the page, providing a cleaner, more professional look.

### 2. Category-Specific Fields
Each world building category now features customized form fields tailored to that specific type of element. No more generic "Description" and "Details" for everything!

## Category Details

### **Location**
When you create a Location, you'll now see fields specifically designed for building places:
- Location Name
- Geography & Climate (terrain, weather patterns, natural features)
- Population & Demographics (who lives there, how many, what groups)
- Local Culture & Customs (traditions, social norms, way of life)
- Economy & Resources (production, trade, resources)
- Government & Power Structure (rulers, organization)
- Notable Landmarks (important buildings, monuments, natural wonders)
- Historical Significance (important events that happened there)
- Connections to Story (how this location relates to your plot)

### **Culture**
For developing societies and peoples:
- Culture Name
- Core Values & Beliefs (what they value, religious beliefs)
- Social Structure (class system, family structure, gender roles)
- Traditions & Rituals (ceremonies, celebrations, rites of passage)
- Language & Communication (language characteristics, dialects, writing)
- Arts & Expression (music, art, literature, performance)
- Food & Cuisine (traditional foods, dining customs, taboos)
- Clothing & Fashion (traditional dress, status symbols, practical wear)
- Taboos & Restrictions (what is forbidden or frowned upon)

### **Magic System**
For creating robust magical frameworks:
- Magic System Name
- Source of Power (where the magic comes from)
- Who Can Use It (innate, learned, or granted; who has access)
- How It Works (casting methods, gestures, words, materials)
- Capabilities & Applications (what it can do, common uses)
- Limitations & Costs (limits, costs to use)
- Rules & Laws (fundamental rules, what's impossible)
- Social Impact (how magic affects society, regulations)
- Dangers & Consequences (what can go wrong, side effects, corruption)

### **Technology**
For developing technological elements:
- Technology Name
- Description (what it is, how it looks)
- Function & Purpose (what it does, problems it solves)
- How It Works (technical details, power source, operation)
- Requirements & Resources (what's needed to create/use it)
- Availability & Access (who has it, how common)
- Advantages & Benefits (what makes it useful or powerful)
- Limitations & Drawbacks (limits, weaknesses)
- Impact on Society (how it has changed the world)

### **Historical Event**
For documenting important events in your world's history:
- Event Name
- When It Occurred (date, era, or time period)
- Where It Happened (locations where the event took place)
- Key Participants (who was involved: leaders, groups, nations)
- Causes & Background (what led to this event)
- What Happened (sequence of events, key moments)
- Outcome & Resolution (how it ended, who won/lost)
- Consequences & Legacy (long-term effects, how it changed things)
- Significance to Story (why this is important to your narrative)

### **Custom**
For any other world building elements:
- Element Name
- Description
- Details
- Connections
- Notes

## How to Use

1. **Open StoryForge.html** in any modern web browser
2. Navigate to the **World Building** section from the left sidebar
3. Click on one of the category buttons at the top:
   - **Location** - for places and settings
   - **Culture** - for societies and peoples
   - **Magic System** - for magical frameworks
   - **Technology** - for technological elements
   - **Historical Event** - for important historical moments
   - **Custom** - for anything else
4. Click the **"Add [Category]"** button to create a new element
5. Fill in the category-specific fields that appear
6. Your data is automatically saved to your browser's local storage

## Technical Information

**File Type**: Single-page HTML application (PWA)  
**Browser Compatibility**: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge (latest versions)  
**Offline Support**: Yes (via Service Worker)  
**Data Storage**: Browser Local Storage  
**File Size**: ~600 KB  
**Dependencies**: None (completely standalone)

## Installation

No installation required! Simply:
1. Extract the ZIP file
2. Open `StoryForge.html` in your browser
3. Start writing!

You can also:
- Bookmark the file for easy access
- Add it to your browser's home screen (on mobile)
- Copy it to a USB drive for portable use

## Data Management

Your story data is stored locally in your browser. To backup your work:
1. Click the **Export** button in the sidebar
2. Choose "Export as PDF" to save your story

**Important**: Your data is tied to the browser and location where you opened the file. If you move the HTML file or use a different browser, you'll need to export and import your data.
